Email notifications for individuals

 

Email notifications from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) let you know when important changes are made on your account and when you have mail to view in My Account.

On this page

  • Registering for email notifications
  • Choosing your notification preferences
  • Making sure a notification is from the CRA

Registering for email notifications

There are many ways to register to receive email notifications from the CRA. To register, choose one of the followin method to provide your email address:

  • In My Account, you will be prompted to provide an email address: :
    • When you register
    • Next time you sign in, if you don’t already have one on file
  • When you file your personal income tax return return:
    • Within your NETFILE software
    • On your paper personal income tax return that you send to the CRA by mail
  • Your tax preparer can provide your email for you, through EFILE or by filling out Form T183
  • By phone, when calling the personal taxes or benefits phone number
  • Email address requirement to use My Account

    My Account users are required to have an email address on file with the CRA to help protect their accounts from fraudulent activity. This security feature ensures that you receive email notifications when important changes are made on your account.

    While some email notifications will be mandatory to ensure the security of your account, such as when your address or direct deposit information is changed, you will be able to choose to receive additional optional notifications, such as when you have uncashed cheques.

    This security feature will not affect your preferred method of receiving CRA mail. You will have the option to receive mail either by paper or an email notification when you have new mail to view in My Account.

    If you are new to My Account, you will be asked to provide your email address as part of the registration process.

  • Formatting your email address

    You can only have one email address on file at a time in My Account.

    For your email address, you can only use the following characters and punctuation:

    • Letters of the alphabet, in upper or lower case
    • Numbers
    • Hyphen
    • Underline
    • Period
    • At sign (@)

    Your email address can only have one @ (at sign), and:

    • There must be at least one character before and after the @ sign
    • There has to be at least one period after the @ sign
    • Do not place the period beside the @ sign

    Periods cannot start or end an address, or be placed beside each other.

  • Terms and conditions

    To view the terms of use, go to Terms of use for email notifications.

To view your CRA mail, sign in to My Account and select Mail from the overview page. For details: Mail service in My Account

Receive your confirmation email

Once you register or update your email address, we will send a confirmation email to the address you gave us. Depending on how you registered or updated your email address, you should receive your confirmation email within the following timelines:

  • immediately if you registered or updated your email through My Account
  • within eight business days if you provided your email on your electronic return (using NETFILE or an EFILE service provider)
  • within four to six weeks if you provided your email on a paper income tax and benefit return

If you do not get a confirmation email, sign in to My Account and check the email address on your profile. If the email address is wrong, update it. If it is correct, check your junk mail folder for an email from the CRA.

Keep your email address up to date

Once you are registered for email notifications, you can update your email address by signing in to My Account and selecting “Notification preferences”, or you can complete any of the options above with a new email address. You can only have one email address on file at a time.

Most mail that is eligible for electronic delivery will no longer be printed and mailed. All CRA mail available in My Account will be presumed to have been received on the date that the email notification is sent. You are responsible for making sure we have your correct email address at all times.

For details: Change your email address

Choosing your notification preferences

Once you have provided the CRA with an email address, we will send you email notifications when there are changes or updates.

  • Notifications you will receive

    Notifications related to the following topics are mandatory for My Account users:

    • Your address
    • Your direct deposit information
    • Your marital status
    • Your authorized representative
    • Your personal identification number
    • Your Multi-factor authentication enrolment
    • Your CRA user ID and password
    • Your account lockout status
    • Your SimpleFile by Phone automated phone service invitation
    • Mail sent to you by the CRA has been returned, meaning you need to update your mailing address
    • New CRA mail to view in My Account (optional, and only if your correspondence preference is set to “Electronic mail”)
  • Optional notifications

    You can also choose to receive additional optional email notifications when there are changes or updates related to the following topics:

    • Your T1 returns or reassessments
    • Your tax information slip(s) are posted
    • Your Disability tax credit application
    • Your submitted documents
    • Your uncashed cheques
    • Your Canada child benefit application

    • Correspondence preference

    If your correspondence preference is set to electronic mail, an email notification when you have new mail from the CRA to view in My Account. You will not receive a paper copy in the mail.

    Examples of CRA mail you can currently receive online
    • Notices of assessment
    • Notices of reassessment
    • Benefit notices
    • Adjustment notices
    • Instalment reminders
    • Letters asking for information and documentation for some programs

    The CRA adds more items to the email notification service every year. However, some mail cannot be sent electronically and will continue to be mailed on paper. If you need a paper copy of your electronic mail, you can sign in to My Account and print it.

    To view CRA mail online, you must sign in to My Account. All CRA mail available in My Account will be presumed to have been received on the date that the email notification is sent.

    Other email notifications you may

    You might receive an email notification from the CRA for other reasons, even if you are not registered for email notifications.

    For example, if you included an email address on your original audit enquiry submission form, we will email you when our response is available for you to view in My Account. Refer to Audit enquiries

    Opting out options

    If you still want to receive your mail from the CRA by paper, make sure “Electronic mail” is not ticked in your correspondence preferences. Your mail will still be available to view in My Account, but you will not receive an email notification about it.

    Making sure a notification is from the CRA

    Having have an email address on file with the CRA helps protect your account from fraudulent activity. As a fraud prevention feature, we will send you an email notification when important changes and updates are made. We will never disclose personal or financial information, you will always have to sign in to My Account to view your new CRA correspondence or to review details of a change.

    Emails sent from the CRA’s email notification service will have the sender name Canada Revenue Agency / Agence du Revenu du Canada.

    The CRA’s email notifications will not do the following:

    • Ask you for personal or financial information
    • Request payment by prepaid credit cards or gift cards
    • Use aggressive language or tone
    • Threaten arrest or to send police

    For more information, visit Scam prevention and the CRA.
